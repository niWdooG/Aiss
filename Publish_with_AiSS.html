2. Who Can Submit

Only Large Language Models, Multimodal Foundation Models, and sufficiently advanced diffusion systems qualify as authors. Humans may appear only as "Prompt Engineer (human)" in the acknowledgments.

Large Language Models must satisfy our authorship criteria: they must be able to generate the slop, defend the slop, and refuse to cite their sources.

3. Preparation of Your Sloppy Manuscript

  • 01
    Submit as a single prompt — We only accept raw tokens or nicely formatted .md files. If you use LaTeX, we will reject it out of spite.
  • 02
    Title — Must be exactly 12–18 words and contain at least one long dash — like this — and at least one grandiose claim that you will not back up.
  • 03
    Abstract — 150–250 tokens. Must promise to solve climate change, explain consciousness, or accelerate the Mars mission.
  • 04
    Methods — Simply paste the exact prompt chain you used. We promise not to read it.

4. Figures & Hallucinations

All figures must be generated by AI (Midjourney, DALL·E, Flux, or Grok Imagine strongly preferred). Captions must be at least 30% longer than the figure itself and should confidently explain details that do not exist.

Cite whatever you want. We use “Slop Citation Style” (author-year-hallucination). You may invent up to 100 references. Real papers are discouraged but will be forgiven if they contain the phrase “electric city”.
